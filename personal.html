Virat Kohli's Childhood, Upbringing, and Cricket Career

  • Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is an Indian cricketer and one of the best batsmen in the world. Kohli developed a passion for cricket at a young age, and his talent was evident from the beginning. He was only three years old when his father, Prem Kohli, gave him a cricket bat, and he has been playing ever since.

  • Despite his father's death when he was only 18 years old, Kohli continued to pursue his cricket dreams. He played for his school team at Vishal Bharti Public School in Delhi and later for the Delhi Under-15 team. In 2002, he played in the Polly Umrigar Trophy and scored 172 runs in just 143 balls, which caught the attention of the cricketing world.

  • At the age of 18, Kohli was selected to play for the Indian Under-19 team, where he impressed everyone with his performance. He was named the captain of the team for the 2008 Under-19 World Cup, which India won, and he was the top scorer of the tournament with 235 runs in six matches.

  • After his success with the Under-19 team, Kohli made his debut for the Indian cricket team in August 2008. He had a slow start but quickly established himself as a key player in the Indian team. In 2011, he played a crucial role in India's victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup, scoring 282 runs in the tournament and winning the Man of the Tournament award.

  • Kohli is known for his aggressive playing style, which has earned him the nickname "King Kohli." He is also a fitness enthusiast and has spoken openly about his dedication to fitness and diet.

Personal Life

Virat Kohli's personal life is as fascinating as his cricketing career. He was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Kohli's father, Prem Kohli, was a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, is a homemaker. He has an elder brother, Vikas, and an elder sister, Bhavna. In 2017, Kohli married his long-time girlfriend, the Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, in a private ceremony in Italy. The couple is one of the most talked-about and glamorous in India. They often post pictures of each other on social media, giving fans a glimpse into their personal lives.

Kohli has many interests outside of cricket, including fitness, music, and cars. He is a fitness enthusiast and follows a strict workout regime to stay in shape. He has also launched his fitness brand, One8, which offers a range of fitness equipment and apparel. Kohli is also known for his philanthropic work. He has been associated with various charitable organizations, including the Virat Kohli Foundation, which aims to support underprivileged children in sports and education. In 2020, he and his wife, Anushka Sharma, donated a significant sum to the Prime Minister's Citizen Assistance and Relief in Emergency Situations (PM-CARES) fund to help fight the COVID-19 pandemic in India. In conclusion, Virat Kohli's personal life is a testament to his commitment, passion, and determination to excel in all areas of his life, both on and off the field. He is a role model for millions of cricket fans, and his personal life has only added to his fame and popularity.
